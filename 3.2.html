Hocco’s Experience Layer

Hocco’s emotional branding formula creates an immersive brand world, the Hoccoverse, with relatable “Scooperheroes” mascots and a playful mission to “freeze time” with ice cream. This approach emphasizes experiential marketing through multi-sensory ads (e.g., mango-scented newspaper ads, 3D mango tree installations) and consistent, joyful digital experiences that foster lasting emotional connections. Key components include:

  • The Hoccoverse: A fictional universe with mascot characters like Choco Champion, engaging Gen Z with a “super chilled-out mission to end urgency with ice cream.”
  • Experiential & Sensory Marketing: Nostalgic packaging (e.g., gift-style boxes, mango-shaped products) and sensory ads create memorable experiences.
  • Digital Engagement: The Hocco website, an “ice cream wonderland,” uses playful typography, unique illustrations, and interactive elements for a joyful user experience.
  • Consumer-Led Strategy: Insights drive innovative products (e.g., sugar-free range, O-cone packaging) and encourage user-generated content for organic reach.
  • Emotional Resonance: Consistent messaging across touchpoints builds trust, creating feelings and memories, not just products.

Quick Commercials Boost Sales

Quick commerce platforms like Blinkit, Zepto, and Instamart contribute 20%% of Hocco’s revenue, up from 7–8% last year. On-ground teams drive growth, but these platforms are game-changers, with Hocco targeting a 25%% contribution in the coming years.

Hocco’s Powerful Comeback

Backed by the experienced team, Hocco has rapidly built a loyal customer base. With 50+ company-owned outlets and 100+ franchise parlors, the brand is achieving 40% year-on-year growth. Its focus on quality, innovation, and emotional branding drives improving revenue numbers, targeting Rs 400 crore by FY26 and Rs 1,000 crore by 2028.
