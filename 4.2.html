Overview

This chapter explores Hocco’s Go-To-Market (GTM) strategy, integrating innovation, marketing, and distribution to drive sustainable growth in the Indian ice cream market. Learn how Hocco uses character-led storytelling, agile product launches, and a balanced marketing mix to capture diverse audiences and counter hustle culture with a “chilled out” narrative.

Hocco’s Go-To-Market Playbook

Hocco’s GTM strategy focuses on innovation, robust logistics, multi-channel distribution, and targeted marketing in the premium ice cream segment. Key elements include:

  • Innovation and Product Differentiation: Driven by “what’s new?”, Hocco creates unique products like the mango-shaped “Aamchi” and O-Cone, with 70-80% classic portfolio and 20% cultural innovations.
  • Robust Logistics: 70% investment in temperature-controlled supply chains ensures quality and scalability.
  • Multi-channel Distribution: General trade (80%), quick commerce (8-10%, up to 20-25%), parlors (10%), and pushcarts (e.g., 1,400 in Delhi).
  • Targeted Marketing: Digital-first with Instagram reels and influencers; hyperlocal campaigns like “Hocco on Wheels”; experiential tactics such as scented ads and inflatable hoardings.
  • Activity: Draft a 30-day strategy for “Chikki-Coffee Crunch” using digital channels and quick-commerce.

Challenges and Insights

Hocco faces supply chain logistics and quality maintenance during expansion. Founder insights highlight innovation to counter competition.

Revenue Channels

Diverse channels include general trade, quick commerce (20% sales), parlors for feedback, and pushcarts for branding.

Hocco Eatery and Creamery

Offers blended flavors with Jain options; creameries as experience centers for sundaes and feedback.

 

Go-To-Market Strategy Details

The Hoccoverse: Mascot-Based Storytelling

Five mascots (Ace, Bud, Chiller, Dash, Eezo) humanize the brand, driving campaigns and collaborations for community engagement.

Innovation as the USP

“What’s new?” leads to viral products like Amchi, with scented ads and hoardings amplifying reach.

Marketing Mix: Digital and Traditional

Balances digital for awareness and traditional for trust; agile budgets under 10% of spend.

Scarcity drives innovation, like our single mango tree hoarding reaching millions online.

- Roli Srivastava, Vice President of Marketing, HOCCO in conversation with Social Samosa

Influencer and Product Strategies

Organic influencer buzz; products like BIX and Amchi designed for shareability among Gen Z.

Quick Commerce and Creameries

Quick commerce (20% sales) for trends; creameries for experiences. O-Cone optimized for delivery.

Regional Expansion and Growth

Expansion to new markets with hyperlocal strategies; FY24 revenue ₹34 crores, targeting ₹500 crores by FY26.

  • Manufacturing: Double to 2.5 lakh liters/day with new plant.
  • Retail: 15,000 to 30,000-35,000 touchpoints.
  • Parlors: Add 100 to reach 250.
  • Geographic: South, East India, UP.
  • International: North America, 10-12 stores.
