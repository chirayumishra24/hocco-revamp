Hocco Business Marketing Strategy

Hocco’s premium sub-brand Huber and Holly offers Sicilian Pistachio, Pondicherry Vanilla, and Coconut Macaroons, supported by automated cone-making machines to meet rising demand.

Expanding Portfolio

Hocco’s 150 SKUs balance classics like Vanilla with innovative products like sugar-free ice creams, O-cone ball-top cones, cake sandwich ice creams, and Huber and Holly’s premium flavors (e.g., Salted Caramel Popcorn, Sicilian Pistachio).

“We’re launching Huber and Holly’s premium flavors like Pondicherry Vanilla and Macadamia Nut in retail by February,” says Chona.

Quick Commercials Boost Sales

Quick commerce platforms like Instamart, Zepto, and Blinkit generate 7-8% of Hocco’s revenue, expected to grow to 20-25% in coming years.

Hocco’s Powerful Emergence

Led by Ankit Chona’s experienced team, Hocco has built a reliable customer base and growing revenue, leveraging his experience in Ice Cream Brand and legacy as a new player in the ice cream market.

Consumer Preferences: Traditional vs. Premium

Traditional Preferences

Classic flavors like Mango Dolly, Malai Kulfi, and Butterscotch appeal to families and older consumers for their cultural connection and authenticity.

Premium Preferences

Urban youth prefer exotic flavors like Biscotti and Sicilian Pistachio, alongside luxury products like Magnum-style bars and ice cream cakes.

Market Implications

Hocco balances traditional staples for loyalty and premium innovations for growth, using digital marketing for shareability.

Evolving Taste and Healthier Segments

Evolving Taste Trends

Urban consumers embrace fusion flavors like Charcoal Lychee and festival-inspired Modak-shaped ice creams, blending novelty with cultural relevance.

Healthier Segments

Hocco’s sugar-free range and clean-label products (no vegetable oils or palm fats) cater to the growing health-conscious segment (~2% of market).

Implications for the Market

Balancing indulgent and healthier products, Hocco uses quick commerce to test and scale offerings for evolving consumer demands.
