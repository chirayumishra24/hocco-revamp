Hocco’s Virtual Internship – Supply Chain Management

The Adventure Begins

Every scoop of Hocco ice cream travels a long, chilly adventure before it reaches your hand. In this chapter, we step into the shoes of Hocco’s supply chain team and follow their story. Hocco Ice Cream uses a variety of ingredients depending on the flavor, but common sourced components include milk solids, sugar, water, and emulsifiers/stabilizers (such as INS 322, 471, 407). Specific flavors incorporate ingredients like cocoa solids/dark chocolate, nuts (cashews, almonds), fruit extracts (mango, lychee), and brownie/biscotti pieces. Artificial and nature-identical flavorings and colors are also used in various products.

The Factory Awakens

Our journey begins at the buzzing factory floor, where machines hum and tubs, cones, and novelties roll out. The first challenge: ensuring capacity keeps pace with growing demand. The Head of Manufacturing checks if production lines are balanced, while the COO scouts new regions for plants closer to customers.

The Cold Caravan

Freshly made ice creams must travel in a frozen caravan. Refrigerated trucks, smart freezers, and 3PL partners guard the products against heat. The Logistics Head watches temperature logs like a hawk, ensuring no scoop ever melts on the road.

Many Roads to Delight

From here, ice creams take three exciting routes: to parlours where families gather, to retail freezers in bustling shops, and to quick-commerce hubs where someone’s midnight craving is just 15 minutes away. Each route has its own hero — Franchise Ops, Trade Marketing, and the E-Commerce Lead — keeping things smooth.

Guardians of Quality

The QA team acts as guardians. They log spoilage, trace every batch, and prepare backup plans in case of power cuts or logistic hiccups. Their mantra: zero waste, zero compromise.

The KPI Treasure Map

To guide the adventure, the team follows a treasure map of KPIs. Each number tells a story — whether ice creams stayed frozen, whether parlours were stocked on time, or how quickly products reached shelves.

KPI Goal Owner
Temperature Excursions < 0.5% Logistics Head
On-Time In-Full (OTIF) ≥ 95% Franchise Ops / Supply Chain
Inventory Days on Hand ≤ 14 days Inventory Manager
Spoilage Rate < 2% QA Manager
Time-to-Shelf ≤ 48 hrs Supply Chain Analytics

Glossary

3PL

Third-Party Logistics: Outsourcing logistics and transportation services to specialized providers.

OTIF

On-Time In-Full: A KPI measuring the percentage of orders delivered on time and complete.

Spoilage Rate

The percentage of products that become unusable due to damage or expiration.

The Adventure Continues

Every month, the team revisits the checklist and updates the map. For interns like you, this is where learning becomes real: observing the flow, spotting bottlenecks, and suggesting improvements. Because in supply chain management, every small step keeps the adventure alive — and every adventure ends with a smile and a scoop.
